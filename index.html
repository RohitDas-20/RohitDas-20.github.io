N.O.E.D.
A fast-paced 3D Multiplayer shooter game

Like most shooter games, FPS games involves an avatar, one or more ranged weapons, and a varying number of enemies.Because they take place in a 3D environment, our games tend to be somewhat more realistic than 2D shooter games, and have more accurate representations of gravity, lighting, sound and collisions. Our FPS game is controlled with a combination of a keyboard and mouse. This system has been claimed as superior to that found in console games,which frequently use two analog sticks: one used for running and sidestepping, the other for looking and aiming. Our game is mostly combat based with emphasis on Movement skills of the Player.It can be very fast paced , the game mostly revolves around the gameplay of the player and the opponent play style. Level Design is very complex it has open spaces ,roof tops, closed areas, street and different height to provides not only more play style options but also encourages player to adopt more complex play style variety Multiplayer is present with room creation and player invite options, etc. It is Free-to-play game and can be enjoyed by our gaming community for literally free of cost.

Movie Recommender System
in R

The main goal of this machine learning project is to build a recommendation engine that recommends movies to users. This R project is designed to understand the functioning of a recommendation system. I developed an Item Based Collaborative Filter. This helped me gain experience of implementing my R, Data Science, and Machine learning skills in a real-life project. Dataset used I have used the MovieLens Dataset. That data I have used consists of 105339 ratings in the ratings.csv file, applied over 10329 movies in the movies.csv. Essential Libraries recommenderlab, ggplot2, data.table and reshape2.
